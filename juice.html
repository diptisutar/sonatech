Juice Clarifier

A juice clarifier is a crucial piece of equipment in sugar mills, used to purify sugarcane juice by removing impurities such as fine particles, fibers, and colloidal matter. This purification process involves adding chemical flocculants to clump together the suspended particles, allowing them to settle to the bottom of the clarifier. The clarified juice, free from impurities, is then drawn off from the top and sent to the next stage of the sugar production process. Different types of clarifiers, including conventional, high-rate, and disc clarifiers, are used depending on the specific needs of the sugar mill. Factors such as flocculant dosage, pH control, temperature, and retention time significantly influence the performance of the clarifier. By effectively removing impurities, juice clarifiers play a vital role in ensuring the production of high-quality sugar.
