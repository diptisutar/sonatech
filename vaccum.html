Vaccum Filter

Vaccum Filter

Vacuum filters are essential equipment in sugar mills, used to remove excess moisture and impurities from the mud or bagasse. This process involves a rotating drum partially submerged in a slurry of mud or bagasse. As the drum rotates, a vacuum is applied to the filter cloth, drawing the liquid through the cloth and leaving behind a solid cake. The filtered liquid, known as filtrate, is collected and further processed, while the solid cake is discharged and often used as a fuel source. Vacuum filters significantly improve sugar recovery by maximizing the extraction of sugar from the mud or bagasse, and they also contribute to efficient water management in sugar mills.
